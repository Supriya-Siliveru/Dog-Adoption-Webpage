Freaquently Asked Questions

How can I adopt a pet from your shelter? +

Visit the shelter, meet the animals, and complete an adoption application. Spend time with the pets you're interested in, ask questions, and be prepared for a home visit or reference check.

What are the adoption fees, and what do they include? +

While the adoption fee is free, the services typically included, such as spaying/neutering, vaccinations, and microchipping, are still provided. This is a wonderful opportunity to give a loving home to a pet in need without the financial barrier of an adoption fee. It's a win-win situation for both the pet and the adopter, as it allows more animals to find caring homes.

Do you offer pet training or behavioral support after adoption? +

These services may include training classes, resource materials, behavior helplines, consultations with experts, and even follow-up visits to ensure a smooth transition for both the pet and the new owner

How can I become a volunteer at your shelter? +

Becoming a volunteer at a pet shelter is a wonderful way to make a positive impact on the lives of dogs in need.Your time and dedication can make a significant difference in providing care and love to shelter animals.

How are the donated funds used by your shelter? +

These funds often go toward the daily care of animals, including medical care, feeding, and routine check-ups. Shelter maintenance, adoption programs, and outreach efforts to find forever homes for animals are also common beneficiaries of donations.

What should I feed my newly adopted pet? +

Feeding your newly adopted pet requires attention to their species, age, and size. If you adopted your pet from a shelter or rescue, consult with them for initial dietary recommendations.
